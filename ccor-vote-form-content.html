This is an official member voting form regarding the proposed association name change from Bonita Springs–Estero REALTORS® to Coconut Coast Organization of REALTORS® (CCOR).

Before you vote, please review the information below so you have full context.

Watch our short video explaining the vision behind CCOR.

Why leadership is recommending this change

This proposal is part of long-term strategic planning built from member input, market research, and feedback—designed to honor our 60-year history while positioning the association for the next 60 years. The goal is to more accurately reflect how members and consumers already experience our market as a connected coastal region, with Bonita Springs and Estero at its center, while reducing unnecessary geographic limitations that can create confusion or barriers as our reach has expanded.

Why "Coconut Coast"?

Coconuts were introduced to our area in the late 1800s by the Koreshans, and Southwest Florida historically included coconut and pineapple farming. While the coconut is not a prominent commercial crop today, it remains a recognizable symbol of our local lifestyle and identity—and it influenced well-known landmarks and infrastructure such as Coconut Point, Coconut Road, and other established place names that connect and represent our broader region.

Why can't we just pick something generic like "SWFL REALTORS®"?

Association names that include the REALTOR® marks are not chosen in isolation. NAR requires an established name-change procedure, including documentation and review, and the proposed name is subject to notice and potential objections through NAR's process. Because neighboring associations and the broader REALTOR® organization have defined jurisdictions and established identities, a broad regional name can create conflicts, overlap concerns, or confusion—and other organizations have the right to object under the NAR procedure. That's why this proposal had to fit within what we're allowed to do—not just what sounds intuitive.

NAR requirements and member-vote documentation

Per NAR's published procedure, associations are only authorized to use names approved through NAR's established process. The required checklist includes:

  1. A formal name-change application signed by the Association Executive
  2. An "Agreement to Use the Term REALTOR®" signed by the association's President and Secretary (and then executed by NAR after approval)
  3. Minutes documenting the membership or board action authorizing the change

(National Association of REALTORS®)

Timeline and collaboration (how we got here)

This has been a structured, multi-phase effort designed to meet NAR requirements while reflecting member feedback:

Early 2025
Board Vote Voted to pursue a name change based on strategic plan feedback
Spring 2025
Marketing Partner Engaged Priority Marketing for research, naming & design
Early Summer
Name Selection After 12 rounds, Board selected CCOR and presented to members
Mid-Summer
Tagline Finalized "The CORE of Real Estate Connections" after 5 rounds
Dec 2025
Brand Identity After 12 rounds, finalized logo and visual identity

Overall, this process took months of collaboration over more than a year to fit within what we are allowed to do, reduce the chance of conflict or confusion, and incorporate the voices and perspectives of members and leadership along the way.
